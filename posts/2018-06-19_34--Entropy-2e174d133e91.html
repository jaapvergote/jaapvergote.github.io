34. Entropy

June 19, 2018

Things tend to gain disorder over time if there is no tight organization in place. Bureaucracy is a great example of a rigid system to counter disorder. It is also inefficient and slow.

A bit of entropy is good, it allows for serendipity and new discoveries. But you have to keep a basic level of organization to move forward.

It is easy to make things complex, it’s hard to make or keep things simple.

By Jaap Vergote
