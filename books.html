Bookshelf

Sometimes people ask me what books I'm reading or what they should read. I made this simple overview to help them pick their next book. It's tough as everyone's preferences are different. The order does not indicate preference.

  • The windup bird Chronicles - Haruki Murakami (I love Haruki Murakami, and believe this is his best work)
  • The hard thing about hard things - Ben Horowitz
  • How to win friends & influence people - Dale Carnegie
  • Man in the Dark - Paul Auster
  • What I talk about when I talk about running - Haruki Murakami
  • Into thin air - Jon Krakauer
  • Walt Disney - Neal Gabler
  • Losing my virginity - Richard Branson (I found a signed copy of this book on a Ryan Air flight, how ironic)
  • The Pixar touch - David A. Price
  • Shoe Dog - Phil Knight
  • A Brief History of Time - Stephen Hawking
  • Start with why - Simon Sinek
  • Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance - Robert M. Pirsig
  • Le tour du monde en 80 jours - Jules Verne
  • Delivering Happiness - Tony Hseih
  • Flow - Mihaly Csikszntmihalyi
  • The call of the wild - Jack London
  • Bright lights, big city - Jay McInerney
  • Thinking fast and slow - Daniel Kahneman
  • Influence - Robert B. Cialdini
  • The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People - Stephen R. Covey
  • The Black Swan - Nassim Nicholas Taleb
  • Man's search for meaning - Viktor E. Frankl
  • Made In America - Sam Walton
  • Antifragile - Nassim Nicholas Taleb
  • Principles - Ray Dalio
  • Ender's Game - Orson Scott Card
  • The Three Body Problem - Liu Cixin
  • Venture deals - Brad Feld & Jason Mendelson - Book on VC and how startup equity works. Recommend everyone considering working/already working at a startup to read this.
  • A long walk to freedom - Nelson Mandela
  • eBoys - Randall Stross
  • Alibaba - Duncan Clark
  • Longitude - Dava Sobel
  • Homegoing - Yaa Gyasi
  • Creative Selection - Ken Kocienda
  • Becoming - Michelle Obama
  • The score takes care of itself - Bill Walsh - Build systems, not goals.
  • The Arsenal of Democracy - AJ Baime - how the Ford Motor Company went from building cars to producing a bomber a day during WW2
  • Why We Sleep - Matthew Walker
  • The Goal - Eli Goldratt - manufacturing!
  • Empires of Light - Jill Jonnes - the battle between Edison, Westinghouse and Tesla at the end of the 19th century
  • The Outsiders - SE Hinton - Very insightful book on how operator-capital managers can allocate cash in their business
  • One up on Wall Street - Peter Lynch
  • Never split the difference - Chris Voss
  • How Google Works - Eric Schmidt
  • Life 3.0 - Max Tegmark
